FRUIT TREE IN MODESTO

California. Owner Jim Rogers insisted that no bareroot fruit tree sapling leave the nursery without a pruning cut that took off the top two-thirds of the little tree. Jim wanted happy customers. He taught us that a person who went home with an unpruned fruit tree would soon be the caretaker of a disappointment: a substandard tree, ungainly and erratically fruitful, that would grow beyond a customer’s capacity to manage it. The tree would develop into an emphatic reminder of both a personal garden failure and the failure of the nursery to help that customer with what he or she needed to know.
